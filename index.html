About me

I am a second year student at University of Michigan majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Mathematics. I have a wide range of interests and thus I value multidisciplinary pursuits. I am passionate about building software, as evident from the software projects I have completed both in and outside of schoolwork and learning new technologies, frameworks and data structures to integrate into what I build. I am also very interested in solving problems for organizations and companies especially relating to areas like optimization in processes and decision-making through use of technology and data processing techniques. In fact, I am currently researching on agent-based modeling for optimization in distributed systems. In addition to software and research, I am also very interested in entrepreneurship and making a positive social impact through it, as seen from my contributions in a non-profit starting organization Bridge Burma (bridgeburma.ml) and a student organization Optimize that promotes entrepreneurship on campus. Lastly, I value creativity over speed and I like to take my time to deliver quality work. Some of my personal strengths are compassion and adaptability, while my biggest weakness is my fear of rejection.
